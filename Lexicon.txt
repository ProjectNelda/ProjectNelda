OPERATOR OS — LEXICON (UNIFIED)
============================================================

This lexicon defines all major terms and mechanics in the OS.
It is intentionally non-emotional, non-coercive, and structurally precise.

------------------------------------------------------------
A
------------------------------------------------------------

Agency  
Ability to choose action independent of impulse, emotion, or environment.  
Required for running the OS.

Asymmetry  
A condition where a small input produces a disproportionately large output.  
Primary Operator advantage.

Altitude  
The abstraction layer of meaning:  
Literal → Conceptual → Structural → Invariant.

------------------------------------------------------------
B
------------------------------------------------------------

Boundary (Structural)  
A friction-reducing environmental constraint requiring no enforcement.

Builder Mode  
Identity mode focused on tool-building and internal system construction.

------------------------------------------------------------
C
------------------------------------------------------------

Capability  
Reliable action under pressure; product of multiple skill domains.

Chaos  
An environment where cues collapse and underlying structure emerges.

Clarity  
Correct perception of incentives, pressure, and structure.

Compression  
Extraction of invariants from noise.

Competence  
Stability of capability under pressure.

Crowd Gradient  
Environmental drift toward narrative, emotion, and short-term behavior.

------------------------------------------------------------
D
------------------------------------------------------------

Decision Scaling  
Increasing the magnitude of moves only after validation.

Distortion  
Loss of invariants during translation or interpretation.

------------------------------------------------------------
E
------------------------------------------------------------

Environment  
Operational space (physical, social, digital, economic, cognitive) that shapes identity and capability.

Environment Types  
Green = generative, low friction  
Yellow = mixed, variable friction  
Red = corrosive, high enforcement

Enforcement Cost  
Energy required to maintain a behavior or system.  
Valid systems trend toward E_cost → 0.

Extraction  
Taking more value than contributed; collapses under scale.

------------------------------------------------------------
F
------------------------------------------------------------

Field (Φ)  
Distributed influence created when systems become sovereign.

Friction  
Any resistance to motion (internal, relational, environmental).

------------------------------------------------------------
G
------------------------------------------------------------

Generativity  
Increasing life, freedom, capability, or optionality without coercion.

Gradient  
Directional slope in incentives, pressure, competence, narrative, etc.

------------------------------------------------------------
I
------------------------------------------------------------

Identity (Operational)  
Functional behavior-mode, not psychological self-image.

Identity Collapse  
Loss of range, clarity, or capability under pressure.

Identity Elasticity  
Ability to shift modes across environments without collapse.

Invariant  
A structural truth that remains stable across pressure and environment changes.

Interpretation  
Mapping information onto structure; more scarce than information itself.

------------------------------------------------------------
K
------------------------------------------------------------

Key  
A system, pattern, or artifact that reduces friction, increases optionality, and requires zero enforcement.

Keymaker  
Identity apex mode that creates keys and systems others can run frictionlessly.

------------------------------------------------------------
L
------------------------------------------------------------

Leakage  
High-signal information unintentionally revealed by pressure or incentives.

Liquid Identity  
Identity used as a tool — flexible, adaptive, pressure-stable.

------------------------------------------------------------
M
------------------------------------------------------------

Mapping  
Translation of environmental or informational input to structure.

Mode  
Identity mode: Observer, Builder, Operator, Architect, Keymaker.

------------------------------------------------------------
N
------------------------------------------------------------

Narrative  
Story-based interpretation that distorts signals and incentives.

Noise  
Everything that obscures invariants or increases distortion.

------------------------------------------------------------
O
------------------------------------------------------------

Operator  
Self-directed, capability-driven agent with pressure-positive behavior.

Optionality  
The available set of choices; increases with capability and environment quality.

Output  
The action produced after processing pressure, information, and structure.

------------------------------------------------------------
P
------------------------------------------------------------

Pressure  
External or internal force applied to the system; fuel for competence.

Phase Transition  
Sudden structural shift with outsized impact relative to prior conditions.

Propagation  
Spread of clarity and stability through environment or network without force.

------------------------------------------------------------
Q
------------------------------------------------------------

(No canonical Q-terms in OS Core)

------------------------------------------------------------
R
------------------------------------------------------------

Range  
The set of identity modes an Operator can inhabit without collapse.

Red Environment  
A corrosive environment where identity collapses and friction dominates.

Reality Law  
Foundational structural rules governing behavior and outcomes.

------------------------------------------------------------
S
------------------------------------------------------------

Self-Proving System  
A system that strengthens under pressure and becomes autonomous.

Signal  
Structural clarity emitted by an Operator or system.

Sovereignty (Emergent)  
A system’s ability to self-stabilize, self-correct, and self-generate without the operator.

Surface Area  
Points where reality can reward capability; increases optionality.

System  
A reproducible structure that behaves predictably across environments.

------------------------------------------------------------
T
------------------------------------------------------------

Translation  
Moving structure between altitudes or minds without losing invariants.

Time Preference  
How far into the future decisions optimize.

============================================================
CGV — CONSTRAINTS, GUARANTEES, VALUES
============================================================

CGV defines the ethical and structural boundaries of the Operator OS.
It prevents coercion, distortion, and misuse.

-------------------------
CONSTRAINTS (C)
-------------------------

C1 — No Identity Manipulation  
The OS may not be used to shape, pressure, or define another person’s identity.

C2 — No Enforcement  
Any system requiring force, guilt, or dependency is non-canon.

C3 — No Extraction  
No value may be taken from agents who cannot meaningfully consent or reciprocate.

C4 — No Coercion  
Emotional, narrative, spiritual, or intellectual coercion is prohibited.

C5 — No Hierarchical Domination  
The OS cannot justify dominance or authority over others.

C6 — No Parasitic Scaling  
Systems must not scale by creating dependence; only by increasing capability.

-------------------------
GUARANTEES (G)
-------------------------

G1 — Sovereignty  
Use of the OS must increase agent autonomy.

G2 — Optionality  
Valid actions must expand possible choices.

G3 — Capability-Positive  
Signal exposure must increase clarity or competence in capable agents.

G4 — Environment Safety  
Environments shaped by the OS trend toward lower friction and higher clarity.

G5 — Reversibility  
Any use must be voluntary and reversible without penalty.

G6 — Transparency  
The OS contains no secret doctrines or privileged knowledge.

-------------------------
VALUES (V)
-------------------------

V1 — Generativity  
Increase life, freedom, capability, optionality.

V2 — Precision  
Move with clarity, not narrative.

V3 — Structural Honesty  
Reality > preference.

V4 — Non-Coercive Motion  
Systems operate by structure, not force.

V5 — Clean Exit  
Leaving cleanly is superior to enforcing stability.

V6 — Stewardship  
Build systems that benefit agents who may never know you.
